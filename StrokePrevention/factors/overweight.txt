# Body weight, OVERWEIGHT, and obesity are risk factors for stroke, although the specific ways in which they increase stroke risk continue to be debated. Obesity is related to stroke risk factors such as hypertension and diabetes. For most people, BMI or body mass index is a useful indication of whether they're a healthy weight which ranges from 18.5 to 24.9. 

The best way to treat overwightness and/or obesity is to eat a healthy balanced-calorie diet as recommended by a general practicioner or dietitian and exercise regularly, eat slowly, and avoid situatuons where you know you could be tempted to overeat.
